Topic Name

Kep point

Set the customer at ease as soon as they say they have a return by assuring them they can get a refund.

Try to elicit information about why they are making the return so that we can identify problems or issues, but don’t make it feel like an interrogation.

Feel free to share a similar story of a return you had to make for the same reasons to build repoire and make them feel less self-conscious

Determine if they are eligible for a full refund because they have a receipt and the item was bought within 30 days. Otherwise they are eligible for an exchange or store credit.

If they are not happy with the options you give them and insist on a refund, offer to have your manager discuss it with them.

Look for indicators of fraud, especially when the customer does not have a receipt, but do not make any accusations. Bring concerns to manager.

Give it a try

An older female customer gruffly throws two dresses on the counter and says she wants her money back. She does not have a receipt but the tags are still on the dresses and they are from your store.

Here are some ways others have handled this situation:

Immediately elevate it to the manager because you can see the woman is not going to accept your options

This approach helps to avoid making her more agitated, but it is entirely possible you can defuse the situation on your own and make her happy.

Tell her you are sorry the dresses didn’t work out, but that you will take care of her. Ask inquisitively why she wants to return them. Tell her about a similar experience with an item of clothing you bought. Explain that you can give her a gift card for the full amount she paid for the dresses.

This is a great way to try to build repoire with the customer and hopefully make her feel positive about the experience and you, even if you are not able to give her a cash refund.This approach helps to avoid making her more agitated, but it is entirely possible you can defuse the situation on your own and make her happy.

After she puts the dresses down and asks for a refund, you ask immediately if she has a receipt to determine what the return options are. When she says no, tell her matter of factly that the store’s return policy does not allow for cash refunds without a receipt.

You may not go well. You have not built any repoire with the customer and instead have immediately opened the door for conflict. She may be fine with a gift card, but by announcing she can’t get a cash refund, she may be offended at the assumption she was expecting something else.

After you have determined that she doesn’t have a receipt, but you see the tags still on, you immediately alert your manager because you suspect fraud.

This would be hasty and may upset the customer. Even if you immediately notice some fraud indicators, take a few moments to try to put the customer at ease and ask a few questions to help you determine if fraud really is likely, then you will have more information to provide to the manager if you still suspect fraud.

Checklist

  • Assure the customer they will get a full refund or exchange
  • Ask what the reason for the return is
  • Determine what refund options they qualify for
  • Ask if they would like to exchange or get a refund
  • Fill out return slip, ask customer to fill out their name and address
  • Place item in return bin
  • Provide customer refund, gift card, or exchange, as well as a coupon for a future sale.
